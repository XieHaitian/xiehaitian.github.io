Taiwan
             Feburary 11, 2016 -- Feburary 18, 2016

(Map from Picture This | Gallery)

People crowded inside Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall for a glance of the "changing of guards" performance, which is a highly intriguing must see. The guards on both sides of the statue are changed each hour. We were there to watch the first one of that day on 9AM. The performance was quite remarkable as the soldiers twirl their guns, if only I can see anything through the crowds.

Feburary 12, 2016 (Taipei)

It is from this picture that I find my mother looks a lot like grandpa.

Feburary 12, 2016 (Nantou)

My mother and I at the Sun Moon Lake in the morning. The east side of the lake resembles a sun while the west side resembles a moon, hence the name. The lake is very famous as it appears in our Chinese textbook in elementary school. Together with Ali Mountain, Sun Moon Lake is the most well-known sight in Taiwan for us mainlanders.

Feburary 12, 2016 (Nantou)

Buddha's Light Mountain is one of the major Buddhist organizations in Taiwan. The abbot Hsing Yun is also a very famous monk, even my grandma read his books. He is known for his paricipation in politics, as his support for One China Policy and Kuomingtang party. Some criticize him, but maybe this is just as the sutra says "Whatever is form, that is emptiness; whatever is emptiness, that is form." Anyhow, the temple and the surrounding ground were really clean and gave you the impression of pure land.

Feburary 13, 2016 (Kaohsiung)

The vegetarian food we had at the Buddha's Light Mountain remained fresh in my memory. We had noodles, fried rice, pizza, cheese cake, brownies, and salad. The meal was quite delicious, and also unbelievable that they were all vegetarian.

Feburary 13, 2016 (Kaohsiung)

The lobster was really fresh and we also had raw sea urchin for that meal, though not so tasty for me. Sea food abound in Taiwan, but the way Taiwanese cook them is too light for inlanders.
